Fragments in Process

An Immersive Virtual Reality Experience Centered Around the Ignite Series
Presented by the Brown Arts Institute. Click to learn more!

I can't take credit for the Lindemann's architectural glory, but the lighting, materials, and rendering in these shots were all me. As a creative designer on this project, my job was to elevate Brown University's Lindemann Performing Arts Center into a surreal, liminal space that could be explored in VR. Turning the real world into the virtual world was not easy. Combining Blender and Unity workflows, I made every polygon count. To make the virtual Lindemann look like its real life counterpart, I created procedural shaders in Unity that both added to the space's aesthetic appeal and optimized for VR.

Thinking In VR

Particle Plants

The leaves and plants in this video are made using a Unity particle system, but believe it or not, so are the sun rays! To mimic real-time raytracing, a rendering process that is pretty but not practical in VR, I combined a particle system with layers of shaders.

Shader Sea

Telling the VR processing system to simulate every single drop of water in an ocean would quickly overwhelm it. Convincing water is still possible. To trick a flat plane into looking like an ocean, I overlapped displacement maps and applied a highly specular and translucent material.

Edge Detection Algorithm

Fragments in Process is a highly experimental endevour, so I wanted to experiment with non-photorealistic looks in VR. This shader, meant to mimic comic book inking, detects differences in an object's color or topology, and if a large enough difference is noticed, it marks an outline.

Character Animation

3D Rigged Character: Teri

Teri is not an iguana. He's an extinct reptile from the Triassic called a Longisquama.


  • Inspired by retro comic books, I shaded Teri with a main shadow, hatching, and stippled scale details. This stylized look is achieved by seperating multi-colored lighting into red, blue, and green channels. Red light casts the main shadow, green light creates the hatching, and blue light stipples the scale details.
  • Teri's facial features are seperate from his head topology. His eyes, mouth, and teeth can all deform on their own. This allows for him to be much more expressive without breaking his topology. This is a loose implementation of this paper from Pixar.
  • To get a 2D ink look, I used deformable curves for Teri's eyebrows and mouth. The eyebrow curves are totally seperate from the mesh, while the mouth curve is shrinkwrapped on. Each curve has multiple control points to allow for twisty, cartoonish lines.
3D Rigged Character: Morgan

Morgan is my first fully first rigged and retopologized human model.

  • I focused on functional retopology for this model. This mesh is made entirely from quads, and the eyelashes, eyebrows, and mouth are integrated into the topology.
  • Morgan's shaders map data from a principled BSDF to a customizable color ramp for a soft, cel-shaded look.
  • Morgan's hair is styled into soft beach waves. It moves dynamically with the rest of the mesh.
